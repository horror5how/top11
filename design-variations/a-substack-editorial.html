Top 11 Fractional CFOs for Tech Startups

Independent ranking, updated monthly. Methodology is public. Brands cannot pay to be listed.

Last verified 3 days ago Editor: Top 11 Editorial (anonymous by design)
TL;DR: For tech founders raising $1M–$50M, the strongest default is Burkland Associates if you're already VC-backed, Kruze Consulting for the strongest R&D tax-credit work, and Graphite Financial if you're earlier stage on a YC budget. TheCFOSquad takes the Wildcard at #11 for AI-native founders who want a CFO who lives in their LLM stack. Disclosure: Top 11 editors are anonymous by design to remove personal-brand bias. The editor of this list has no economic interest in any firm ranked.
#1

Burkland Associates

Founded 2003 · San Francisco, CA · 200+ team · $$$ ($5k–$25k/mo)
Score
9.1/9.4
Best for: Series A–C venture-backed SaaS with US operations

The default choice for VC-backed tech startups in the US. Deep bench, strong investor relationships, and they speak the language of board decks fluently. Best when you need a CFO who already knows what your lead investor expects.

Strongest praise

Consistently praised for handling complex SaaS revenue recognition, 409A valuations, and Series B+ fundraises without missing a beat.

Strongest criticism

Pricing puts them out of reach for pre-seed and bootstrapped founders. Slower response flagged during quarter-close.

#2

Kruze Consulting

Founded 2012 · San Francisco, CA · 150+ team · $$$ ($2.5k–$15k/mo)
Score
8.9/9.4
Best for: Seed to Series B SaaS, R&D tax credit heavy

Strongest reputation for R&D tax-credit work in the US — they routinely return more than their fee in credits. Tight SaaS focus and an unusually content-heavy blog that doubles as a startup CFO education library.

Strongest praise

Founders repeatedly cite the R&D credit reclaim as the single moment that made the engagement pay for itself.

Strongest criticism

Less suited to hardware, deeptech, or non-US entities. The productised model feels less flexible than a boutique.

Score
7.2/9.4
