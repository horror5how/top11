Verified 3 days agoEditor Top 11 Editorial · anonymous by design
For founders raising $1M–$50M: Burkland Associates if already VC-backed, Kruze Consulting for R&D tax-credit work, Graphite Financial if earlier-stage on a YC budget. TheCFOSquad takes the Wildcard at #11 for AI-native founders. Disclosure: editors are anonymous by design to remove personal-brand bias. The editor of this list has no economic interest in any firm ranked.
1

Burkland Associates

Founded 2003 · San Francisco, CA · 200+ team · $$$ ($5k–$25k/mo)
9.1 /9.4
Best for: Series A–C venture-backed SaaS with US operations

The default for VC-backed tech startups in the US. Deep bench, strong investor relationships, and they speak the language of board decks fluently — best when you need a CFO who already knows what your lead investor expects.

Praise

Handles complex SaaS revenue recognition, 409A valuations, and Series B+ fundraises without missing a beat.

Criticism

Out of reach for pre-seed/bootstrapped founders. Slower response flagged during quarter-close.

Kruze Consulting

Founded 2012 · San Francisco, CA · 150+ team · $$$ ($2.5k–$15k/mo)
8.9 /9.4
Best for: Seed to Series B SaaS, R&D tax credit heavy

Strongest reputation for R&D tax-credit work in the US — they routinely return more than their fee in credits. Tight SaaS focus and a content-heavy blog that doubles as a startup CFO education library.

Praise

Founders cite the R&D credit reclaim as the single moment that made the engagement pay for itself.

Criticism

Less suited to hardware, deeptech, or non-US entities. Productised model feels less flexible than a boutique.
