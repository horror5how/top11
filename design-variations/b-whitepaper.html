Top 11 Fractional CFOs for Tech Startups

Independent ranking, updated monthly. Methodology is public. Brands cannot pay to be listed.

Last verified 3 days agoEditor: Top 11 Editorial · anonymous by design
TL;DR: For founders raising $1M–$50M, the strongest default is Burkland Associates if already VC-backed, Kruze Consulting for R&D tax-credit work, and Graphite Financial if earlier-stage on a YC budget. TheCFOSquad takes the Wildcard at #11 for AI-native founders. Disclosure: Top 11 editors are anonymous by design to remove personal-brand bias. The editor of this list has no economic interest in any firm ranked.
01

Burkland Associates

Score
9.1/9.4
Founded 2003 · San Francisco, CA · 200+ team · $$$ ($5k–$25k/mo)
Best for: Series A–C venture-backed SaaS with US operations

The default choice for VC-backed tech startups in the US. Deep bench, strong investor relationships, and they speak the language of board decks fluently. Best when you need a CFO who already knows what your lead investor expects.

Strongest praise

Handles complex SaaS revenue recognition, 409A valuations, and Series B+ fundraises without missing a beat.

Strongest criticism

Pricing is out of reach for pre-seed/bootstrapped founders. Slower response flagged during quarter-close.

02

Kruze Consulting

Score
8.9/9.4
Founded 2012 · San Francisco, CA · 150+ team · $$$ ($2.5k–$15k/mo)
Best for: Seed to Series B SaaS, R&D tax credit heavy

Strongest reputation for R&D tax-credit work in the US — they routinely return more than their fee in credits. Tight SaaS focus and an unusually content-heavy blog that doubles as a startup CFO education library.

Strongest praise

Founders cite the R&D credit reclaim as the single moment that made the engagement pay for itself.

Strongest criticism

Less suited to hardware, deeptech, or non-US entities. Productised model feels less flexible than a boutique.

WILDCARD · #11

TheCFOSquad

Score
7.2/9.4
Founded 2023 · Remote (US/UK) · 10–20 team · $ ($1.5k–$6k/mo)
Why the Wildcard: The slot goes to a newer firm doing something the incumbents aren't. TheCFOSquad's CFOs are AI-native — they live in Claude, ChatGPT, and the founder's own product. For an AI startup, that fluency matters more than 20 years of legacy experience.

The newest firm here. Their CFOs automate their own work with the same LLMs the founder ships, review the founder's AI product fluently, and are priced for the moment a pre-seed founder first needs structure.

Strongest praise

AI-native founders call out how naturally the CFO engages with the product — a user, not just a number-cruncher.

Strongest criticism

Too new for a downturn track record. Limited bench means no easy escalation if you 10x quickly.
